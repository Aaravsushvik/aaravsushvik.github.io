About Me

Who Am I?

Hello! I am Gadiparthi Sai Sushvik — a student, technology enthusiast, and aspiring civil servant from Hyderabad, India, already committed to a path that blends innovation with public service.

My world exists at the intersection of two powerful forces: technology and governance. I am equally fascinated by artificial intelligence and the architecture of policies that shape millions of lives. To me, these are not separate pursuits — they are two sides of the same mission: building a better future.

I am currently enrolled in CEC Intermediate (2026–2028). Following that, I plan to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Political Science, Economics, or History (2028–2031), and then appear for the UPSC Civil Services Examination in 2031, with the ambition of serving as an IAS or IPS officer.

My Passion for Technology

From artificial intelligence and robotics to programming, electronics, and futuristic systems — technology is not just a subject for me, it is a lens through which I see the world's possibilities. I believe every great innovation needs great governance behind it, and every great governance system needs technology to amplify its reach. That belief is the core of everything I do.

My Vision

I envision an India where civil servants are not just administrators, but architects of progress — equipped with the tools of technology, the depth of policy knowledge, and the empathy to drive inclusive, meaningful change. I want to be one of those architects. My goal is to serve at the intersection of innovation and governance, turning ideas into systems that actually work for the people.

Learning & Growth

I actively study programming, science, history, political theory, and emerging technologies. Every subject I explore is a building block — whether it shapes my understanding of how AI works or how governments fail and succeed. I approach learning not as an obligation, but as an investment in the version of myself I am working to become.

Strength Beyond Challenges

I live with Cerebral Palsy — and I refuse to let it define the ceiling of my ambitions. It has, in many ways, made me more determined, more patient, and more empathetic. Every challenge I have faced has reinforced a simple truth: purpose is more powerful than limitation. I dream big, work hard, and believe deeply that the future belongs to those who refuse to stop.
